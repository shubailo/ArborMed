# ArborMed: 3 Feature Ideas

Based on the core philosophy of "Cozy Competence", the Adaptive SM-2 engine, and the Gamified Economy of ArborMed, here are three new feature proposals:

## 1. Cooperative "Study Wards" (Collaborative Multiplayer)
**Concept:**
While the current "Duel Mode" offers PvP competition, many students find competition stressful (which goes against the "Cozy" philosophy). "Study Wards" would allow up to 4 students to form a persistent, collaborative study group.
**Mechanics:**
- **Shared Clinical Cases:** The group is presented with a complex, multi-step clinical scenario (Bloom Level 4). Members must discuss and vote on the correct diagnosis/treatment path.
- **The Ward Room:** The group shares a cooperative "Ward Room" that they can decorate together.
- **Rewards:** Completing daily "Ward Rounds" grants "Ward XP" and exclusive, large-scale decor items that can only be placed in the shared Ward Room.

## 2. Ambient "On-Call" Pomodoro Timer
**Concept:**
Students often need to study from textbooks or external lectures but want to stay immersed in the ArborMed ecosystem.
**Mechanics:**
- **The Mode:** A built-in Pomodoro timer that displays the student's customized "Clinic Room" in a fullscreen, ambient mode.
- **Audio:** Plays curated "Medical Lofi" or ambient soundscapes (e.g., rain on a clinic window, distant soft paging, rhythmic EKG beeps).
- **Rewards:** Students earn a slow, passive trickle of Stethoscopes (e.g., 5 coins per completed 25-minute focus session) and maintain their daily streaks, encouraging them to use ArborMed as their primary study companion even when not taking quizzes.

## 3. Daily "Patient Shift" (Narrative Case Studies)
**Concept:**
Currently, questions are served individually. This feature introduces a cohesive narrative element to the daily study loop.
**Mechanics:**
- **The Shift:** Once a day, the student can clock in for a "Shift." They are assigned a single, evolving virtual patient.
- **Progression:** The shift consists of 5-10 sequential questions spanning multiple Bloom levels. For example: Patient presents with symptoms (Level 1/2) -> Order tests -> Interpret EKG (Level 3) -> Prescribe treatment -> Handle a sudden complication (Level 4).
- **Rewards:** Successfully stabilizing the patient awards a significant XP multiplier, a "Shift Complete" stamp, and occasionally a "Clinical Supply Crate."
